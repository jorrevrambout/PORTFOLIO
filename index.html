Motivational letter

Why scenography.

Summarise your previous trajectory in one sentence.

Born in Brussels, raised in Mechelen, and having completed a Master's degree in Product Development in Antwerp over the past five years, I have gradually positioned myself at the intersection of design, sustainability, technology and human experience.

How do you function in a team?

I am naturally a curious, goal oriented and open minded team player who enjoys taking initiative, bringing forward ideas, connecting with people and creating new collaborations. I am enthusiastic and like to bring energy and momentum to projects, while being comfortable both working independently and contributing within a professional team environment. Where I sometimes encounter challenges is in the shared vision for the final outcome. When the level of ambition, commitment or underlying vision differs significantly within a team, it can create friction later in the process. I have also learned that I am a perfectionist, which sometimes makes it difficult to delegate. However, through the many collaborative projects throughout my Product Development education, I have grown significantly in working within multidisciplinary teams and balancing individual responsibility with collective goals.

What is your vision of scenography today?

I see scenography as something that extends far beyond spatial design alone. It is about creating a complete experience and shaping the emotions people feel when participating in an event. This begins long before the event itself, through the careful development of a clear identity, vision and purpose. The anticipation leading up to the event already becomes part of the narrative. From there, the story continues through the interactivity, originality and atmosphere that the event offers, with the visitor's sense of freedom, energy and engagement placed at the centre. In contemporary events, I often notice a constant search for originality, for that unexpected element that gives an experience its own character. At the same time, it is important not to lose sight of clarity and coherence. The challenge lies in balancing a carefully designed user experience with opportunities for visitors to think differently, explore creatively and temporarily enter a small utopian world. For me, the story does not end when the event is over. A successful experience continues to live on in the thoughts, memories and conversations of its participants long afterwards.

How will you divide your time next year?

Alongside my passion for designing physical experiences and services, my master's thesis has opened the opportunity to continue conducting research. Half of my time will be dedicated to the development of the RepairHub Antwerp project, a collaboration between the University of Antwerp and Stormkop. The project aims to design and test an inclusive repair ecosystem that encourages people to repair products rather than replace them. My role focuses on designing the user experience, services, interactions and physical environment surrounding this repair hub.

Time division continued

In parallel, this project may become the starting point of a PhD trajectory investigating the role of Agentic AI within the circular repair economy. The research explores how intelligent digital agents can support repair services, improve accessibility and contribute to a more sustainable society. Alongside these commitments, I hope to further develop my knowledge of scenography and spatial design through this programme, particularly within the context of festivals, cultural events, concerts and immersive experiences. Equally important is the opportunity to meet like minded people, establish new collaborations, continue experimenting independently and build a pathway into the complex and often difficult to enter event industry.

What would you build if budget were not a factor?

If budget were no limitation, I would not immediately start building. Instead, I would first search for an extraordinary and exclusive location within Belgium. My ideal project would be a multi day underground festival situated in an abandoned industrial site in Charleroi. The atmosphere would be intimate and immersive. Through collaborations with climbing communities and slackliners, temporary climbing structures would be integrated throughout the site. The industrial architecture would be softened and enriched through greenery, natural elements and warm colours. The festival would offer an immersive experience without relying on large technological screens, using projection mapping, lighting, sensory interventions and site specific artworks created from found materials instead.

The dream build continued

Rather than promoting major commercial headliners, the focus would be on emerging local artists who express their genre in its purest form. The musical highlights would be carefully curated through live performances that reinterpret genres in unexpected ways, such as house music performed by a live band, jazz ensembles or techno translated through classical instruments. Visitors would discover secret pop up stages and move through different musical worlds that tell a story spanning from the 1960s and 1970s to the late 1990s. Inclusivity and small scale would remain central throughout. The architecture itself would become an active part of the experience. Water, heights, underground spaces, rooftops and hidden passages would continuously reshape the visitor journey, creating unexpected moments and encouraging exploration at every turn.

B|YOU is a cohesive tool collection inspired by the dreamy world of creation, where freshness, elegance and modernity meet. Flowing lines and clean contours give the family a look that feels energetic and in motion, while a vibrant signature blue and refined details give every tool a fresh, contemporary character.

  • Ergonomics
  • Branding
  • Tool family
  • User research
  • SolidWorks

Every tool carries an integrated, labeled loop so it can be hung and recognised at a glance, keeping the workspace calm and organised. The family holds four specialised tools. KUNZIET, a hand drill built for smooth operation and precise depth control. OPAAL, a jeweler's file with seamless grip contours for fluid shaping. ONYX, ergonomic pliers that pair strong function with an elegant, high control handle. CALCITE, a jeweler's saw made to turn creative ideas into flawless actions.

CALCITE, the empowering jeweler's saw

Designed as a core piece of the family, CALCITE helps craftspeople turn ideas into action. Beyond its modern look it focuses on ergonomics and adaptability for long hours of precision benchwork. The handle tilts upward to reduce the twisting angle of the wrist and lower fatigue. A specialised grip texture sits on the pressure points between the middle, ring and pinky fingers for maximum control. A smart sliding frame makes the saw fully modular so it adapts to blades of different lengths, and an integrated tightening mechanism tensions the blade with ease for controlled, precise cuts.

Biloo is an off grid sanitation solution that improves comfort and sustainability at crowded music festivals. It is a waterless duo compost toilet that saves space by sharing one 330 litre sawdust dispenser and a single integrated waste tank across two separate cabins. The system runs entirely without electronics. A manual flush mechanism releases a precise amount of sawdust through a custom rotor profile to keep odor and moisture under control. Built for heavy festival use, it combines a robust AISI 304 stainless steel frame with durable, weather resistant HPL panels, and the final design can be transported, stacked and placed with standard forklifts, so there is no on site assembly and far less maintenance.

RepairAid is my master's thesis at the University of Antwerp, within Product Development. It explores how artificial intelligence can support a more accessible and circular repair culture. Today many household devices are thrown away while repair is still technically possible. The problem is often not the fault itself but the uncertainty around it. People do not know what is wrong, how much a repair will cost, how long it will take or which options exist, so replacing becomes the easy choice.

  • Circular economy
  • Service design
  • Agentic AI
  • Diagnosis
  • Repair Base

RepairAid removes that uncertainty. It combines historical repair data, user input and agentic AI in one guidance system. Instead of sending people to scattered manuals or search engines, it guides them step by step in understanding a fault and choosing a repair route.

Three routes to a repair

The system distinguishes three routes. Independent repair at home, guided repair in a Repair Base, and professional repair. Based on the user situation, the complexity of the fault and the expected effort, RepairAid suggests the most feasible option, creating a low threshold path that supports both citizens and professional repairers.

The innovation lies in combining circular design thinking with agentic AI. Where AI is often used for efficiency or automation, RepairAid asks how the same technology can serve social and ecological goals. By making repair easier it helps extend product lifetimes, reduce material use and shift behaviour from throwing away toward circular use.

Firma Funk is a side project I cofounded with four close friends, bringing together product development, graphic design, photography, journalism, economics and social work. What began as a shared love for disco, groove and early electronic music grew into a collective focused on intimate events that put atmosphere, connection and storytelling before spectacle.

  • Scenography
  • Event design
  • Vinyl and groove
  • Inclusivity
  • Community
  • Cofounding

Our first event, SILO 1, was built around one idea. A party should feel like a narrative rather than a playlist. We moved away from the harder, louder, faster reflex of much of today's nightlife and designed a gradual musical journey around vinyl sets, groove and careful transitions from day into night.

To bring this to life we transformed Bar Ziek, a former hospital site in Antwerp, into a temporary world of its own, combining indoor and outdoor spaces that visitors discovered through the day. Together with fellow designers we treated scenography as far more than decoration. It is music, light, space, social interaction and the emotional rhythm of an experience.

Inclusivity mattered too. SILO 1 hosted a wearable haptic prototype that let people with hearing impairments feel the music. As cofounder my role was mainly that of a facilitator, connecting people, shaping the vision and bringing many creative disciplines into one coherent experience. I believe an event starts long before visitors arrive and continues long after they leave.
